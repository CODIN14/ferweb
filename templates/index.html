üî¨ Neutrosophic Logic Framework

Truth (T)

Represents the degree of confidence or truthfulness in the detected emotion. Higher values indicate stronger evidence for the emotion.

T = emotion_score / 100
Range: [0, 1]

Indeterminacy (I)

Quantifies uncertainty and ambiguity in emotion detection. High indeterminacy indicates the system is uncertain or emotions are mixed.

I = f(closeness, variance, entropy)
Multi-factor uncertainty

Falsity (F)

Represents the degree of contradiction or absence of the emotion. Complements the truth value to model inconsistency.

F = 1 - T
Contradiction degree
üéì Academic Foundation: This system implements Florentin Smarandache's Neutrosophic Logic, which extends fuzzy logic by explicitly modeling indeterminacy. Unlike traditional emotion detection systems that only provide confidence scores, our neutrosophic approach provides a complete picture: what we know (T), what we don't know (I), and what contradicts our findings (F). This makes emotion recognition more transparent, reliable, and suitable for real-world applications where ambiguity is common.
